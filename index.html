About

The Story So Far

I'm a second-year B.Tech Biotechnology student at KIIT University, Bhubaneswar, working at the intersection of physics, biology, and computation. My journey began with a deep curiosity for molecular mechanisms and has since expanded into the world of data-driven and structure-based biology.

My academic projects include protein–ligand docking studies with AutoDock Vina and molecular dynamics simulations for studying structural stability of protein systems. I apply Python and machine learning for biological data analysis, bringing together statistical modeling and computational tools.

I am strongly motivated to contribute to ongoing research in structural and computational biology, with particular interest in computer-aided drug design, epigenetics, gene regulation, and probabilistic/statistical modeling of biological systems.

Outside of academics, I enjoy exploring interdisciplinary connections between physics, mathematics, and biology — believing the most powerful biological insights emerge at the intersection of these disciplines.
